The Approach

Portrait Photography Rooted in People, Not Technique

The portraits in this gallery were not made by technique alone. They were made by creating the right conditions, a calm atmosphere, a genuine conversation, enough time to let the subject settle, and the patience to wait for something real.

Technical skill matters. Lighting, composition, and timing all contribute to a strong portrait. But they are tools, not the point. The point is the person. A technically perfect image of someone who looks uncomfortable is not a good portrait. A technically simple image of someone who is completely themselves can be extraordinary.

This is the standard every session at Peter Farrar Portraits is held to. Visit the about page to learn more about the experience and approach behind the work, or explore the investment page to discuss your own session.

Patience Sessions are never rushed. Time is taken to make sure every subject feels genuinely comfortable before the camera becomes part of the conversation.
Honesty The goal is always an image that reflects who the subject actually is, not a performance of who they think they should be.
Craft Lighting, composition, and post-production are applied with care and intention. Every image delivered is professionally finished to a consistent, high standard.
Longevity Portraits are created to be displayed and treasured for years, not to date quickly. The style is timeless rather than trend-led.
