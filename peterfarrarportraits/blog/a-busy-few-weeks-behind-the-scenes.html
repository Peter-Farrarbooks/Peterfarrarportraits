A Busy Few Weeks Behind the Scenes

Developing powerful automation tools, planning new projects, and seeking male models for portfolio portrait shoots.

12 June 2026 Photography Tips By Peter Farrar
Home › Journal › A Busy Few Weeks Behind the Scenes

Over the past few weeks, many of you may have noticed that updates on Peter Farrar Books have been a little quieter than usual. The reason is simple, I've been spending a significant amount of time working behind the scenes on some exciting new automation projects that have the potential to transform how I manage my websites, content, and online presence.

As someone who wears many hats across photography, publishing, web development, and community projects, time is always my most valuable resource. I've been developing and testing a range of automation tools designed to handle repetitive tasks, streamline content management, and improve workflows across multiple platforms. While the work has been time-consuming, the long-term benefits will be substantial, freeing up countless hours that can be redirected towards writing, photography, creating new content, and engaging with readers.

The good news is that much of this development work is now starting to come together. Once fully implemented, these systems will allow me to spend less time on administration and more time focusing on the creative projects that matter most. I'm looking forward to sharing more updates soon and returning to regular posts, with plenty of new books, ideas, and projects already in the pipeline.

Alongside this, I'm also looking to expand my portrait photography portfolio and am currently seeking male models for a series of creative portrait sessions. These shoots are completely free and are designed to explore new ideas, locations, and styles while creating high-quality images for both the model and my portfolio. No previous modelling experience is necessary, just confidence, enthusiasm, and a willingness to try something different.

Thank you for your patience and continued support. There are some exciting developments ahead, and I look forward to sharing both the automation projects and new portrait work with you very soon.
