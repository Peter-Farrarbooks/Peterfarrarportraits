The Process

What to Expect at Your Portrait Session

Every session follows the same principle, unhurried, personal, and focused on producing portraits you will be proud to display. Here is what the experience looks like from first contact to final delivery.

Step 01

Initial Consultation

We begin with a conversation, by email or phone, to understand exactly what you are looking for, who the portraits are for, and what style and setting will suit you best. There are no obligations and no pressure.

Step 02

The Session

Your session takes place at the studio in Basingstoke or on location across Hampshire. Sessions are relaxed and unhurried, there is always time to settle in, try different looks, and create something that genuinely reflects who you are. Guidance on posing, expression and composition is provided throughout.

Step 03

Image Selection and Delivery

After the session, your images are professionally edited and prepared for delivery. High-resolution digital files are supplied, ready for printing, framing and sharing. Fine art prints and wall art products are also available. You will only receive images that meet the standard you came for.

To discuss your session or check availability, visit the investment page or get in touch directly.

Experience You Can Trust

Portrait Photography Rooted in Experience

Peter Farrar Portraits is built on more than eighteen years of professional photography experience across the United States and the United Kingdom. Founded in 2008 in Charlotte, North Carolina as Kenneth Light Studios, the business has photographed hundreds of clients across weddings, portraits, commercial work and events.

Since relocating to the UK and rebranding as Peter Farrar Portraits, the same approach that earned five-star client reviews across the USA has been brought to Basingstoke and Hampshire — unhurried sessions, honest communication, and portraits that genuinely reflect the people in them.

Peter is also a published author with books on professional photography and leadership, including The Complete Photography Field Guide, a 538-page reference covering technique, business and UK photography law. Read more about Peter here.
