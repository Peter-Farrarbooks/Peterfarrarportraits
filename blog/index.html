Portrait Photography Journal

Photography Insights, Stories and Inspiration

Welcome to the Peter Farrar Portraits journal, a space for photography inspiration, behind-the-scenes stories, session guides and honest thoughts on what it takes to make great portraits. Based in Basingstoke and working across Hampshire, I write about the craft, the people, and the process behind every session.

Portrait photography is about far more than technical settings. It is about creating the conditions for a genuine moment, the right light, a relaxed subject, and the patience to wait for something real. This journal explores all of that, from the practical to the personal.

Whether you are considering booking a portrait session in Basingstoke, curious about what happens behind the camera, or simply interested in photography as a craft, there is something here for you. New articles are published regularly, covering everything from lighting and location scouting to what to wear for your session and how to feel comfortable in front of the camera.

What to Expect from the Journal

Every article is written by Peter Farrar, a portrait photographer based in Basingstoke with decades of experience photographing people across the UK and USA. The journal covers the full range of portrait photography, from studio lighting techniques to candid location sessions in the Hampshire countryside.

Session stories give you a real sense of what a portrait shoot looks like from start to finish. Photography tips cover practical guidance you can apply whether you are a subject wanting to prepare for your session, or a fellow photographer looking for insight into the craft.

Studio news keeps you updated on what is happening at Peter Farrar Portraits, new availability, upcoming shoots, behind-the-scenes projects and occasional offers for portrait sessions in Basingstoke and across Hampshire.
